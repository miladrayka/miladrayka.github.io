About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Shahid Beheshti University. My research lives at the interface of machine learning, molecular informatics, and computational physical chemistry.

I develop robust machine learning and deep learning architectures tailored to predict complex properties in chemical, bio, and solid-state systems. Moving forward from a strong foundation in biomolecular structures and software toolkits, my current work focuses on expanding into materials informatics to accelerate the discovery of high-performance thermoelectric systems.

Research Focus

Thermoelectric Materials

Applying machine learning models alongside domain data representations to evaluate, optimize, and accelerate the discovery of high-performance thermoelectric solid-state materials.

Biomolecular Scoring & Drug Discovery

Designing predictive models and geometric scoring architectures for high-accuracy protein-ligand binding affinity prediction, virtual screening, and enzyme engineering.

Reliability & Uncertainty Quantification

Integrating uncertainty quantification protocols within deep neural architectures to benchmark model confidence and ensure actionable property predictions across computational discovery pipelines.
