Research Interests

My research interfaces data-driven artificial intelligence, molecular informatics, and quantum chemical physics to resolve complex structural bottlenecks in drug discovery, biocatalysis, and solid-state materials design.

  • AI-Driven Drug Discovery & Biocatalysis: Developing machine learning and deep learning models for protein-ligand binding affinity prediction, automated QSAR/ADMET screening pipelines, and structural enzyme engineering.
  • Machine Learning for Materials Science: Applying machine learning and deep learning architectures to optimize and accelerate the discovery of high-performance thermoelectric materials and predict solid-state properties.
  • Uncertainty Quantification (UQ) Methods: Implementing robust UQ algorithms within neural networks to evaluate prediction reliability and enhance model confidence.

Supervisor: Dr. Shant Shahbazian

Title: Development of Effective Electronic Structure Theory for Muonic Molecular Systems: Hartree-Fock and Kohn-Sham Equations
